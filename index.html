Welcome

I am a physicist turned technical program manager.

I received my PhD in physics from Columbia University in 2015. As a graduate student, I was a researcher and commissioner at the LIGO Hanford Observatory, where I was responsible for getting the detector operational for the first time. Shortly thereafter, we made the first direct detection of gravitational waves, which ultimately won the founders a Nobel Prize. Following my graduate work, I was a software test engineer at a drone start-up called Airware and later joined a quantum computing start-up called Rigetti. At Rigetti, I held various other positions including Quantum Engineer, Deployment Lead, and Technical Lead/Program Manager. I'm currently a Sr. Technical Program manager at a fintech company.

My passion and strength has been integrating several R&D, hardware, and software projects, making such projects production ready, and collaborating cross-functionally. I have managed engineers and scientists to ensure timely progress and that projects come to fruition. In addition, I have also acted as an individual contributor by adding to code base, running measurements, and analyzing data to name a few. I enjoy technical challenges, working with teams, being at the intersection of business and technology, and getting things done. I can't wait to keep learning!
